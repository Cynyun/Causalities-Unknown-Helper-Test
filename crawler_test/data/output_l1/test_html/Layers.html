Layers

⚠️ Spoiler warning

Scav Prototype is organized into layers, each with their own collections of tiles, structures, traps, enemies, and items. There are currently 5 accessible layers in the Itch.io demo build and the 6.x Steam demo.

Description

Each layer is 307.2m tall. The player must go through these layers to progress in the game. There is currently no limit to the amount of layers the player can go through, and the game goes back to deeper gravel lands after overgrown depths is completed.

Upon generation of each layer, there can rarely be a Layer modifier added.

By default, the amount of traps and items changes per layer. This can be adjusted in the main menu.

One block in the world is exactly 0.30 by 0.30 meters and is 1 Unity unit by 1 Unity unit. Gravity in the game is set to 55 Unity units / s², or 16.5 m/s² (including in the Tutorial), which is a bit less than twice Earth's gravity.

Layers in order

Gravel Lands

The gravel lands is a cavernous, cold layer made up of small interconnected caves.

Most of the layer is made of light stone, gravel, soil, and clay, with groundwater pools filling in any gaps. Wooden scaffolding generates around the layer, and are easy to dig through. Climbing Ropes generate occasionally, which can be climbed to reach different sections of the layer. Holes in the background illuminate parts of the layer, providing some lighting.

Only shade crawlers spawn here, and traps are limited to geysers, jump pads, and spike traps. Falls are usually short.

This layer serves as the start for all runs, and will never reappear later in the run.

Deeper Gravel Lands

The deeper gravel lands is a direct continuation of the gravel lands, bearing many of the same characteristics, along with more dangerous hazards.

Unlike the gravel lands, there are no illuminating holes in the background, and wooden scaffolding is replaced by concrete beams. Lumalgae can be found alongside groundwater. Barbed fences, bear traps, and cave tick swarms appear in this layer, along with the hazards from the gravel lands.

Sand and sandstone will appear as the player makes it towards the end of the layer.

  • The game currently loops from this layer once the overgrown depths layer is completed, doing so until the player ends the run (whether forcefully or willingly).

Dried Desert

The dried desert is a desert-themed layer with spacious caves made of sand and hard stones. Tall drops, turrets, coils, stalactites, and landmines make mistakes much more punishing compared to the previous layers, and force the player to look in every direction.

Waste pipes and oil can be found alongside groundwater, the latter of which is rarer to find in the desert. As oil is more viscous and harder to escape in this layer, deaths caused by drowning via fractures or exhaustion poses a new challenge.

However, this layer provides new tools, including very powerful Rose lights, as well as sand vines that can be climbed, similarly to the climbing ropes that appear in the gravel lands. Recharge station structures also generate here, though they are frequently flooded with oil.

Wall biters and Shade crawlers are enemies that spawn.

The layer tests the player's ability to spot hazards, encouraging a slow and careful approach through tall drops, large caves, and obscured areas.

Wasteland

The wasteland is a radioactive fallout-themed layer sharing many characteristics with the dried desert.

While the layer lacks enemies of any kind, sound cannons, toxirock, and spent fuel force the player to decisively route through the layer, whilst spotting and avoiding the dangerous threats from the previous layer.

Durable brick blocks can sometimes be found around the layer, and verdure will start appearing as the player makes it towards the end of the layer.

Overgrown Depths

The overgrown depths also known as The Jungle is a jungle-themed layer filled with thick vegetation.

Skullcrushers, and grabber plants are new traps found in this area. Luckily, sound cannons and other wasteland traps are not present. Every plant thus far besides cacti will spawn here, and quite frequently, making starvation a non-issue.

Because of the abundance of food, drinkable sap and groundwater, and the lack of turrets and landmines, one might mistake this layer for some sort of paradise if it weren't for a very large and very noticeable threat.

Thornbacks, overgrown ticks, cave ticks, and elder thornbacks are enemies that spawn in this layer as well as wall biters, forming the primary challenge.

It is currently the final layer of the game.

IN-DEV Layers

These layers are still in development and aren't accessible to the current Itch.io build or the Steam demo.

Frozen Chasm

The frozen chasm is an in-development ice-themed layer, not currently accessible in the public Itch.io build.

This layer can be accessed if you save upon exiting Layer 5. The main danger planned with the layer is the freezing cold, and to a lesser extent, Snow striders and Gunmines, the player must go between Ion space heaters, Xalorises and life pods to stay warm and not freeze to death

  • The layer at the moment will be blank if entered in the current build (v5.1)
  • The frozen chasm along with Fungal Pools have been fully teased within a stream and video (but are still W.I.P)
  • Normally continuing will loop the player back to the Deeper gravel lands

Fungal Pools

The Fungal Pools are an in-development layer, continuing after the frozen chasm.

This layer is a fungus themed layer, with many fungus themed blocks, traps and enemies. The main danger within the layer is the constant rain that is very likely to make you freeze to death if sleeping while wet, the player must sleep within life pods to avoid this, other dangers include sawblades, Side Stabbers and Grabber Shrooms that can easily cause injuries.

  • The layer isn't able to be loaded in the current build of the game (v7.0).
  • The Fungal Pools and frozen chasm were the 2 layers fully teased within a stream and the Steam trailer (but are still W.I.P).
  • The layer is the only layer that fungali bungali can be found and broken to obtain bunchunk and craft procoagulant.

The layers after 7 will crash when trying to load so do NOT mod your save file to these layers if you don't have a backup to its previous version.

Crystalline Hollow

The Crystalline Hollow is an in-development layer.

This is the same setting as the Crystalline Hollow from Orsoniks' game Gooey

From what footage and text from the locale has surfaced so far, this layer will revolve around making use of various crystals while avoiding others. The main dangers of the layer are the hectic terrain with very very large drops, the different types of crystals and a flying crystal enemy called Manifestation, its also said that the corpses of said enemy, if not consumed quickly, will turn into 2 Manifestations, though the larger the group of them is, the slower they are.

Trivia

  • Tracks for the frozen chasm, fungal Pools, crystalline Hollow and Geothermal Vents can be found in the soundtrack page.
  • Reaching the bottom of a layer prompts the player to either save and quit, or continue to the next layer. This disables the player's ability to jump until an option is chosen, so be ready to leave once you reach the bottom.
    • The player can use the Jetpack to get back off of the bottom of the layer, but this does not remove the save/continue prompt from the middle of the screen or return their ability to jump.
    • No longer true as of version 5 on itch.io and version 6.x on Steam.
  • Any liquid that touches the bottom of a layer disappears.
    • Items do not, however. This is likely to prevent players from losing the items in their hands permanently due to falling.
  • It is possible to enter a different layer in the tutorial.
    • The Tutorial structure does not despawn, but the traps and enemies in a layer may spawn inside it.
    • The Tutorial structure will not spawn if the game is saved and reloaded instead of just continued.
